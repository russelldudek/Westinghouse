# Westinghouse OPX Chief of Staff Candidate Campaign

Candidate campaign for Russell Dudek's application to the Westinghouse OPX Chief of Staff role in Cranberry Township, Pennsylvania.

## Campaign thesis

**Make execution legible before it becomes urgent.**

The campaign proposes an **Executive Operating Envelope**: a role-specific operating model connecting commitments, constraints, decision rights, and learning so executive attention can focus on emerging systemic risk without centralizing every decision or adding unnecessary bureaucracy.

## Artifacts

- `index.html` — candidate vision and interactive operating model
- `resume.html` — exactly two-page printable role-aligned resume
- `cover-letter.html` — exactly one-page printable cover letter
- `interview-brief.html` — interview thesis brief, proof map, 120-day entry plan, objection analysis, executive questions, and sources
- `styles.css` — shared visual, responsive, accessibility, reduced-motion, and print system
- `app.js` — interactive operating-envelope simulator

## Evidence integrity

Career facts are constrained to verified RoleForge candidate evidence. The campaign does not claim direct nuclear-industry experience. Because the full job-description text was not reliably available through public indexing during research, internal-remit assumptions are explicitly labeled as hypotheses for discovery.

## Research basis

Sources are linked in the candidate vision and interview brief and were reviewed through July 11, 2026. Primary sources are Westinghouse publications and the public Operating Plants portfolio; Reuters Events is used as external context for execution and supply-chain pressure.

## Publication
