# Westinghouse AI Governance Campaign - Brand Intelligence

## Campaign relationship

This is an independent candidate campaign by Russell Dudek for Westinghouse Electric Company's Director, AI Strategic Governance role. Westinghouse branding identifies the intended employer. The AI Assurance Lattice and Portable AI Assurance Case are original candidate work and do not imply Westinghouse authorship, sponsorship, approval, employment, or endorsement.

## Color token provenance

The recognition layer reuses values measured from Westinghouse public corporate CSS and documented in the existing Westinghouse brand package: `#253470`, `#12326A`, `#0675BC`, `#1EC2F3`, `#487C09`, `#FDD922`, and `#78272A`. Candidate-original support colors are limited to white/light-neutral surfaces and a subordinate violet `#6B57A5` used only to distinguish the role-specific assurance lattice from prior Westinghouse campaign geometry.

## Typography

Westinghouse public CSS declares Gotham and GothamMedium. These are proprietary fonts, and no font binaries are copied. The campaign uses Aptos Display / Aptos / Segoe UI / Helvetica Neue / Arial as licensed/system substitutes, reproducing the geometric, compact, technical character through weight, hierarchy, tracking, and editorial rhythm.

## Visual translation

- Official blue/white identity, disciplined grids, cyan signals, product imagery, and strong rules create employer recognition.
- The candidate-original AI Assurance Lattice uses orthogonal evidence beams and a stable use-case core rather than the prior Westinghouse Executive Operating Envelope.
- Motion assembles evidence around a use case and settles into a consequence-based operating posture. The official logo remains static.
- Documents use the same token system, thin technical rules, restrained lattice accent, and official wordmark without creating fake corporate letterhead.

## Restrictions

Official marks are used solely for nominative employer identification. Public product imagery does not imply that Russell Dudek created, operated, or owns the depicted product. The campaign avoids official navigation, employee-portal styling, corporate legal furniture, and implied endorsement.
