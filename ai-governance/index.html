Make assurance portable.

Westinghouse does not need governance that merely slows AI down. It needs an operating system that lets credible AI move faster because evidence, authority, and accountability travel with every use case.

Candidate thesis: Build each AI initiative around a reusable assurance case - mission boundary, data pedigree, model evidence, human authority, security and compliance, and outcome telemetry - then route it according to consequence.

One assurance case. Different evidence burden.

The model is deliberately portable. The six evidence dimensions stay stable while depth, reviewers, tests, and operating posture change according to consequence.

These scenarios are candidate hypotheses for discovery, not claims about undisclosed Westinghouse deployments or internal controls.

Illustrative assurance case

Engineering knowledge assistant

Assisted workflow
  1. Mission & boundaryBoundRetrieve and synthesize approved technical knowledge; no autonomous engineering decision.
  2. Data pedigreeReadyApproved corpora, source ownership, document status, lineage, and citation behavior.
  3. Model evidenceBoundGrounded-answer tests, hallucination probes, retrieval coverage, and change-control checks.
  4. Human authorityReadyEngineer accepts, rejects, or corrects the output before it affects work.
  5. Security & complianceBoundRole-based access, export-control handling, data-loss controls, and audit logging.
  6. Outcome telemetryBoundUse, correction, retrieval success, and workflow outcome signals return to the assurance case.
Human authorityEngineer remains the named decision authority; cited source material is visible at the point of use.
Evidence of valueTime to locate approved knowledge, citation completeness, correction patterns, adoption by workflow.

The first 120 days: earn the right to standardize.

Start with evidence and relationships, not a grand governance launch. The goal is to make the current portfolio visible, test the assurance case in live work, and scale only what proves useful.

Days 0-30

Read the system

  • Map AI demand, decision rights, data domains, platforms, control functions, and existing standards.
  • Interview business and technical owners around delays, rework, risk, and adoption friction.
  • Establish a fact base without prematurely grading maturity.
Days 31-60

Define the contract

  • Co-design the assurance-case standard and consequence-based operating postures.
  • Clarify RACI, exception paths, review triggers, and evidence ownership.
  • Select a small set of representative use cases for controlled learning.
Days 61-90

Prove the mechanism

  • Run assurance-case reviews inside active delivery work.
  • Instrument cycle time, evidence gaps, exception aging, human overrides, and adoption.
  • Retire redundant review steps and document reusable patterns.
Days 91-120

Scale the learning

  • Launch the portfolio cadence and business-partner playbook.
  • Publish decision rights, reusable control patterns, and executive scorecard definitions.
  • Set the next portfolio based on demonstrated value and unresolved consequence.

Questions I would bring to the CTO and governance partners.

Where is AI governance currently creating confidence, and where is it creating avoidable cycle time?This distinguishes controls that protect the enterprise from controls whose evidence could be reused or moved earlier.
Which AI decisions must be globally consistent, and which should remain with business, engineering, quality, or regional authorities?This reveals the real federation model and the decisions that cannot be centralized safely.
What evidence would make an executive comfortable funding a pilot, moving it into production, or stopping it?This defines the portfolio scorecard around decisions rather than activity.
How will the AI business partner team be measured: ideas identified, solutions launched, adoption sustained, value realized, or controls improved?This makes the partnership model accountable to operating outcomes.
Which upcoming regulatory or customer expectations will change the minimum assurance case over the next 18 months?This turns regulatory scanning into design input for platforms, data, and workflows.
