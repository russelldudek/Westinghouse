Dear Hiring Committee:

Westinghouse is hiring this role at the point where AI ambition becomes an enterprise operating question: how to scale useful AI across engineering, data, business workflows, and global operations without weakening traceability, cybersecurity, compliance, or qualified human authority. My perspective is straightforward: governance should not be an approval layer added after the build. It should be a reusable evidence system that helps credible work move faster.

That is the argument behind my independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/Westinghouse/ai-governance/. It presents an AI Assurance Lattice: a portable assurance case built around mission boundary, data pedigree, model evidence, human authority, security and compliance, and outcome telemetry. The artifact demonstrates how I would connect enterprise strategy, data governance, risk, business partnership, and measurable adoption without claiming knowledge of undisclosed Westinghouse processes.

My experience combines the adjacent disciplines this mandate requires. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, and customer delivery in high-reliability advanced-electronics environments and built operating controls, root-cause practices, and quality systems. At Amazon, I built scaled operating cadence and escalation mechanisms in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Through DudeWorth, I design AI-readiness, governance, retrieval, evaluation, human-in-the-loop, and adoption frameworks. At Vape-Jet, I now work directly across robotics, telemetry, quality, support, engineering issue flow, ERP, CRM, and AI-assisted workflows.

I do not present that record as nuclear-industry equivalence. I present it as evidence that I know how to enter consequential technical systems with respect, make decision rights and evidence visible, and translate emerging technology into governed work that people can adopt. My first 120 days would focus on reading the existing system, co-designing the assurance contract with the functions that hold real authority, proving it in representative use cases, and scaling only what reduces risk or cycle time without diluting accountability.

Westinghouse's mission demands both imagination and restraint. I would welcome the opportunity to help build an AI and data governance system that protects what must not drift while making the next good investment, deployment, and operating decision easier.

Carpe diem,Russell Dudek
