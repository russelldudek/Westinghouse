First 120 Days: Earn the Right to Standardize

A discovery-led entry plan for enterprise AI strategy, data governance, assurance, business partnership, and measurable adoption.

Entry principles

Respect existing authorityDo not centralize decisions that belong to engineering, quality, security, legal, privacy, regulatory, or business owners.
Start with live workTest governance in representative use cases instead of designing only from policy abstractions.
Measure the mechanismTrack evidence gaps, review latency, exceptions, overrides, adoption, and outcomes before declaring maturity.

Outcome by day 120

A visible AI portfolio; a co-owned assurance-case standard; consequence-based operating postures; clarified decision rights; representative use cases that prove or challenge the mechanism; an AI business-partner playbook; and an executive scorecard with defined units, owners, sources, and decision use.

First 120 DaysRussell Dudek · Days 61-120

Days 61-90 - Prove the mechanism

  • Run assurance-case workshops inside live delivery work with business, data, technical, and independent authorities together.
  • Measure time to evidence, review latency, exception aging, rework, unresolved authority, human overrides, and adoption behavior.
  • Test whether the standard reduces ambiguity and late surprises without creating clerical work that does not change a decision.
  • Convert successful evidence and control patterns into reusable templates, platform guardrails, test suites, and data requirements.
  • Document where the model fails, where authority is unclear, and which controls should be retired, moved earlier, automated, or strengthened.

Days 91-120 - Scale the learning

  • Launch an executive portfolio cadence that distinguishes exploration, qualified pilots, controlled production, remediation, exceptions, and stopped work.
  • Equip AI business partners with an intake and discovery playbook that begins with outcome, workflow, owner, human authority, evidence, adoption, and consequence.
  • Publish the assurance-case standard, operating postures, decision-rights model, exception protocol, and control-pattern library.
  • Set the next portfolio using evidence from the pilots rather than enthusiasm or generic maturity scoring.
  • Establish the regulatory/standards watch and change process for NIST, NRC, ISO/IEC, EU, privacy, cybersecurity, and relevant customer expectations.
