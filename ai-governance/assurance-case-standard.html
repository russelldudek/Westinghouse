Proposed AI Assurance Case Standard

A candidate-created discussion artifact for discovery - not a statement of Westinghouse policy or current internal controls.

Purpose

Create one portable evidence contract for AI initiatives so strategy, data, technology, business ownership, independent controls, and adoption can make faster, more consistent decisions without diluting authority.

Minimum assurance-case record

DimensionRequired questionsEvidence examples
Mission & boundaryWhat decision or workflow changes? What may the system not do? Who owns the outcome?Use-case charter, prohibited uses, process map, consequence classification, accountable owner.
Data pedigreeWhat data is used? Who owns it? Is it fit, lawful, secure, current, and traceable?Catalog/lineage, quality tests, access, retention, consent/purpose, representativeness, source status.
Model evidenceHow does it behave across intended and adverse conditions? What changes require revalidation?Evaluation plan/results, challenge sets, uncertainty, bias, robustness, drift, version and change records.
Human authorityWho reviews, decides, overrides, appeals, escalates, or stops the system?Decision-rights record, user workflow, override/appeal path, training, competency and independence requirements.
Security & complianceWhich cybersecurity, privacy, legal, quality, export, records, regulatory, and customer controls apply?Threat model, access/logging, privacy assessment, control mapping, review decisions, incident/recovery plan.
Outcome telemetryHow will we know the workflow is useful, safe, adopted, and still within its operating boundary?Business/operating measures, use and correction patterns, exceptions, incidents, overrides, drift, benefit evidence.

Design principles

Evidence before scaleProduction posture is earned through a use-case-specific record, not model novelty.
Authority before automationHuman decision rights are explicit before the AI is embedded in work.
Reuse before repetitionApproved patterns, tests, and controls should compound across use cases.

Operating postures and review triggers

PostureIntended useTriggers to move, hold, or escalate
ExploreBounded research, sandbox, discovery, feasibility.Move when outcome/owner/data are qualified; hold for prohibited data/use, no owner, or unclear consequence.
AssistHuman-authoritative recommendations, drafting, retrieval, analysis.Move when grounding/evaluation, user workflow, source visibility, training, and correction path are adequate.
ControlProduction workflow integrated with monitoring and recovery.Requires validation, release/change control, security/privacy, telemetry, rollback, incident and exception handling.
Independent reviewHigh-consequence, regulated, safety, employment, or other sensitive decision support.Requires independent qualified authority, formal assurance, challenge testing, records, strict change control, and explicit stop rights.

Exception record

Every exception should identify the requirement, rationale, accountable approver, affected use case and consequence, compensating control, evidence, monitoring, expiration/review date, and closure decision. Exceptions are portfolio signals: repeated exceptions indicate a missing platform capability, weak standard, or unresolved operating tension.

Portfolio decision record

Fund / pilot

Outcome, owner, readiness, consequence, evidence plan, adoption burden, and learning value are explicit.

Scale / standardize

Operating evidence shows value, control effectiveness, adoption, recoverability, and a reusable pattern.

Remediate / hold

Evidence, data, authority, control, adoption, or operating boundary remains insufficient.

Stop

The outcome is weak, risk cannot be responsibly bounded, ownership is absent, or better alternatives exist.

Executive scorecard definitions

  • Portfolio: work by posture and decision; benefit realization; reuse; stop/remediation decisions.
  • Flow: time to qualified pilot; review and exception aging; rework caused by late evidence.
  • Assurance: lineage/evidence completeness; validation defects; incidents; recovery and closure.
  • Authority: unresolved owners; override, appeal, and stop-path behavior.
  • Adoption: workflow use, training, repeated use, corrections, and responsible user confidence.
  • Learning: reusable patterns, retired controls, updated standards, and new data/platform capabilities.

Targets should be set only after baseline definitions, source systems, units, owners, and decision use are validated.
