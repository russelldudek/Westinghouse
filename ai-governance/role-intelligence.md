# Director, AI Strategic Governance - Role Intelligence

## Nominal role

Lead Westinghouse enterprise AI and data strategy, governance frameworks, data management, risk/compliance, AI business partners, and executive/regulator communication.

## Actual mandate

Turn expanding AI demand into a governed portfolio of adopted enterprise capabilities that create measurable operating value while preserving evidence, human authority, cybersecurity, privacy, quality, export-control discipline, and regulatory trust.

## Company moment

Public Westinghouse materials position AI across reactor design, licensing, procurement, manufacturing, construction, and operating-fleet improvement, alongside major AP1000 growth ambitions and partnerships. This increases the need for secure data, repeatable assurance, clear decision rights, and trustworthy scaling.

## Operating tensions

1. Speed vs evidence.
2. Central standardization vs qualified local/functional authority.
3. AI ambition vs data readiness and stewardship.
4. Innovation throughput vs cybersecurity, privacy, legal, quality, and regulatory assurance.
5. Portfolio activity vs adopted business value.

## Candidate thesis

**Make assurance portable.** Every AI initiative should carry a reusable assurance case covering mission boundary, data pedigree, model evidence, human authority, security/compliance, and outcome telemetry. Evidence depth varies by consequence. The common contract allows governance to accelerate credible scale instead of becoming a bespoke approval queue.

## Operating model

AI Assurance Lattice + consequence-based postures:
- Explore
- Assist
- Control
- Independent assurance review

## Strongest reasonable hiring concern

Russell has not held a nuclear-industry AI governance role. The affirmative transfer case is high-reliability engineering and quality systems, regulated autonomous operations, scientific grounding, AI readiness/governance design, hands-on workflow integration, and scaled adoption - while Westinghouse experts retain nuclear authority.

## First 120 days

Read the system -> define the assurance contract -> prove it in representative work -> scale the learning.

## Factual boundary

Use-case scenarios, internal governance architecture, maturity assumptions, and proposed controls are candidate hypotheses for discovery, not statements about undisclosed Westinghouse systems or practices.
