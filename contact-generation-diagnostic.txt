Scaling ambition requires a stronger operating envelope.
Westinghouse is entering a moment when the opportunity is obvious and the operating burden is easy to underestimate. Publicly, the company is
advancing fleet-scale AP1000 deployment, long-lead supply-chain investment, and a broad operating-plant portfolio spanning engineering, outages,
parts, digital optimization, I&C, staffing, manufacturing, and long-term operations. In that environment, a Chief of Staff can create disproportionate
value by helping the business see commitments, constraints, and decisions early enough to act—without turning executive cadence into more
bureaucracy.

That is work I have repeatedly done from different operating positions. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer
delivery, and complex technical programs in advanced electronics and high-reliability environments. At Amazon, I led launch and execution in a
24/7 customer-backward system supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually, building the routines, visibility, escalation paths,
and standard work required to keep scale from becoming noise. Today at Vape-Jet, I connect production, quality, customer, field, engineering, ERP,
issue-flow, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows into a more coherent operating system.

I have not spent my career inside a nuclear OEM or utility, and I would not pretend otherwise. My case is transferability: high-consequence
technical execution, quality discipline, regulated operations, cross-functional dependency management, and the ability to make complex work
legible to operators and executives. Nuclear expertise should remain with nuclear experts. My contribution would be to make those experts easier to
hear at the right moment, clarify where decisions belong, surface systemic risk earlier, and convert repeated recovery into better mechanisms.

My candidate thesis is an Executive Operating Envelope: a practical system linking a commitment map, constraint ledger, decision architecture,
and learning registry. The point is not a new dashboard. It is to help leaders distinguish normal variance from emerging systemic risk, allocate scarce
expertise deliberately, and retire reporting that consumes attention without changing a decision.

I would welcome the opportunity to pressure-test that thesis against the real OPX operating context. The independent candidate vision makes that
reasoning visible through the operating model, evidence map, strongest objection, executive questions, and a 120-day entry plan designed to learn
before prescribing.
